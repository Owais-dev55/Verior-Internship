Escalation in Israel-Iran Conflict

A ballistic missile strike by Iran hit Soroka Hospital in southern Israel, prompting Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz to declare that Iran’s Supreme Leader “can no longer be allowed to exist.” This marks a significant intensification in the conflict, with concerns over wider regional instability mounting. Israel’s defence minister said on Thursday that Iran’s supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei “can no longer be allowed to exist” after Soroka hospital in southern Israel was hit during an Iranian missile attack, Agence France-Presse (AFP) reports. Israel Katz told journalists in Holon near Tel Aviv: Khamenei openly declares that he wants Israel destroyed – he personally gives the order to fire on hospitals. He considers the destruction of the state of Israel to be a goal, Such a man can no longer be allowed to exist.

Middle‑East Tensions Could Shake Global Economy

At the Times CEO Summit, experts warned that escalating Middle Eastern tensions, particularly threats to Iran’s oil infrastructure, could lead to oil prices surging past $100/barrel. The FTSE 100 already fell amid rising oil and defense stocks, while central banks reassured markets. The Norwegian central bank has delivered a surprise quarter-point cut in interest rate to 4.25 per cent, its first reduction of borrowing costs in five years. “Inflation has declined since the monetary policy meeting in March and the inflation outlook for the coming year indicates lower inflation than previously expected,” Ida Wolden Bache, governor of Norges Bank, said. The currency and Norwegian government bond yields fell. The dollar was up more that 1 per cent against the Norwegian crown. Government bond yields fell after the decision by as much as 10 basis points to 3.95 per cent, the lowest since May.

US Tightens Visa Rules for Students

The U.S. State Department is now requiring foreign student visa applicants to publicly share access to their social media accounts as part of enhanced security checks. The move drew sharp criticism from privacy advocates and coincides with broader geopolitical and domestic policy shifts

Trump Deliberates U.S. Strike on Iran

President Trump approved military plans to strike Iran, targeting key nuclear sites like Fordo, though he has yet to give the final order. The indecision has kept global observers on edge as regional conflict escalates
